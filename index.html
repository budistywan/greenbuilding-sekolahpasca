01 · Problem Framing
The Setpoint as a Decision Under Uncertainty

Operational management of HVAC systems in educational buildings is conventionally reduced to a scalar decision: the temperature setpoint. This reduction is not irrational — it reflects the absence of a system capable of evaluating higher-dimensional alternatives in real time. The setpoint, once fixed, functions as a static proxy for a dynamic optimization problem it was never designed to solve.

The optimization problem it is proxying for has the following structure: minimize aggregate AC energy consumption while maximizing the fraction of rooms within the thermal comfort zone — two objectives that are conflictual by definition. No single setpoint dominates both simultaneously across the full operational day.

Struktur masalah formal
minimize Σᵢ AC_load_i(t) ← EEC objective maximize |{i : |PMV_i(t)| ≤ 0.5}| / N ← IHC objective subject to T_room,i = f(T_out, occ_i, solar_i, SP_i) SP_i ∈ [T_min, T_max] ∀i ∈ {1,...,N}, ∀t ∈ T_operational ───────────────────────────────────────────────── N = 110 ruang · |T_operational| = jam operasional Dua objektif konfliktual: tidak ada solusi dominan

"Static setpoints encode an implicit weighting between energy and comfort that was never explicitly decided, is never reviewed, and cannot adapt to the conditions it is meant to manage."

Bobot implisit dalam setpoint statis
SP* = argmin_SP [ w · EEC(SP) − (1−w) · IHC(SP) ]
dimana w tidak pernah dielisitasi secara eksplisit — tertanam dalam keputusan historis yang tidak terdokumentasi. Sistem yang diusulkan mengeksternalisasi bobot ini menjadi parameter yang dapat dikonfigurasi dan ditinjau.

02 · Variability Analysis
Sources of Variability: Why the Scalar Reduction Fails

The setpoint-as-scalar-decision fails not because of poor calibration, but because the underlying problem is not scalar. Three sources of variability render any fixed setpoint suboptimal across a non-trivial fraction of the operational space.

Temporal variability. Solar irradiance incident on a given facade follows a deterministic function of solar angle — predictable, but varying continuously. In Semarang's geographic position (6.99°S), the diurnal outdoor temperature range reaches 6–8°C on clear days, driving infiltration heat load that a static setpoint cannot compensate for without overcooling at one end or undercooling at the other.

Spatial variability. Two spaces with identical HVAC specifications may exhibit divergent thermal behavior of 1.5–2°C under identical external conditions — from aging seals, different glazing transmittance, duct asymmetry, or AC units operating below rated efficiency.

Occupancy-driven variability. Metabolic heat load scales directly with actual occupancy. A room scheduled for 40 occupants but hosting 12 carries 70% less metabolic load — a thermal discrepancy of approximately 1.5°C that propagates directly into PMV error. This is not random noise; it is systematic and directional.

Kuantifikasi dampak per sumber variabilitas
TEMPORAL ΔT_solar,i(08:00) vs ΔT_solar,i(13:00) → beda hingga 3.8°C fasad aktif Setpoint optimal berbeda → ~1.5–2°C SPASIAL β_solar,i aktual vs default (4.0) → deviasi ±0.8–1.5°C per ruang Tidak dapat dikompensasi → tanpa per-room observability OKUPANSI n_scheduled = 40, n_aktual = 12 ΔT_orang: 2.2°C vs 0.66°C Error thermal → ~1.54°C PMV error → ~0.46 (hampir satu kategori) ───────────────────────────────────────────────── Akumulasi ketiga sumber pada kondisi puncak: error kumulatif bisa mencapai 3–4°C dari kondisi optimal — menempatkan ruang di luar zona nyaman meski setpoint "benar"

03 · Occupancy Estimation
The Occupancy Problem: A Bayesian Formulation

The occupancy estimation problem in educational buildings has a specific structure. Two information sources are available with complementary failure modes: scheduled occupancy provides a deterministic prior — always available, but encoding planned rather than actual presence, with systematic bias toward overestimation. Camera-based counting provides a likelihood update — direct visual evidence at per-minute resolution, but subject to accumulating error from occlusion and detection failures.

The appropriate formulation is Bayesian: the schedule provides the prior distribution over likely occupancy; camera observations update that prior conditioned on detection reliability. When cameras fail, the system degrades gracefully to schedule-based estimates — a property of the design, not an exception handler added post-hoc.

Bayesian occupancy fusion
n̂ᵢ(t) = argmax_n P(n | z_sched,i(t), z_cctv,i(t)) P(n | z_sched, z_cctv) ∝ P(z_cctv | n) · P(n | z_sched) ↑ likelihood ↑ prior kamera jadwal Prior: P(n | z_sched) = 𝒩(μ_sched, σ²_sched) Likelihood: P(z_cctv | n) = 𝒩(ρ·n, σ²_cctv) Posterior analitik: n̂ᵢ = (σ²_sched · z_cctv/ρ + σ²_cctv · μ_sched) ───────────────────────────────────────────── σ²_sched + σ²_cctv
ρ = detection rate per kamera per ruang, dikalibrasi dari ground truth spot-check.   σ²_sched diestimasi dari historical deviation selama fase kalibrasi awal.   Properti kritis: σ²_cctv → ∞ ⟹ n̂ᵢ → μ_sched (graceful fallback saat kamera dropout).
Karakteristik error per sumber
JADWAL (prior) σ²_sched → diestimasi dari historical deviation (fase kalibrasi 2 minggu) Bias arah → overestimate ~20–35% di non-examination periods Failure → acara non-reguler, penggunaan ad-hoc KAMERA (likelihood) ρ → dikalibrasi per kamera per ruang (angle, oklusi berbeda) Error type → accumulating (missed entry/exit propagates as bias) Failure → cahaya rendah, oklusi parah, hardware dropout POSTERIOR σ²_post < min(σ²_sched, σ²_cctv) → fusion selalu lebih baik dari sumber tunggal jika error antar sumber tidak berkorelasi penuh Asumsi yang perlu divalidasi empiris: independence antara error jadwal dan error kamera — mungkin dilanggar saat acara besar non-jadwal

"The fusion framework is designed to be right most of the time, and gracefully wrong the rest."

04 · Adaptive Thermal Model
Per-Room Parameter Estimation: Every Room Has a Thermal Fingerprint

A thermal model initialized with generic coefficients describes how a prototypical room should behave — not how any specific room actually behaves. Two rooms with identical specifications may require different control actions to achieve the same thermal outcome, because their actual thermal parameters diverge from nominal values in ways that are room-specific and not observable from design documents.

The appropriate response is not to discard the physics-based model — its structure captures causal relationships correctly. The appropriate response is to treat its coefficients as unknown parameters to be estimated from sensor observations over time. The thermal load model is linear in the parameters, which makes recursive least squares (RLS) the natural estimator.

RLS dengan forgetting factor λ
θᵢ = [β_solar,i, β_orang,i, β_infil,i]ᵀ (per ruang) φᵢ(t) = [ΔT_solar,i(t), ΔT_orang,i(t), ΔT_out,eff(t)]ᵀ T̂_model,i(t) = θᵢ(t−1)ᵀ · φᵢ(t) Kᵢ(t) = P_i(t−1)φᵢ(t) / [λ + φᵢᵀ(t)P_i(t−1)φᵢ(t)] θᵢ(t) = θᵢ(t−1) + Kᵢ(t) · [T_sensor,i(t) − T̂_model,i(t)] ↑ innovation: selisih prediksi vs sensor Pᵢ(t) = (1/λ) · [I − Kᵢ(t)φᵢᵀ(t)] · Pᵢ(t−1)
λ = 0.98 diusulkan sebagai titik awal — effective memory ≈ 50 observasi.   λ = 1.0: standard LS tanpa forgetting.   λ → 0: hanya observasi terbaru.   Sensitivitas terhadap pilihan λ adalah bagian dari validasi eksperimental.
Interpretasi parameter yang diadaptasi
β_solar,i default: 4.0 ↓ turun jika: kaca tinted, ada overhang, orientasi tidak aktif ↑ naik jika: kaca bening, fasad langsung terekspos β_orang,i default: 0.055 °C/orang ↑ naik jika: ventilasi buruk, densitas lebih tinggi dari nominal ↓ turun jika: ruang berventilasi baik, occupancy konsisten rendah β_infil,i default: 0.35 ↑ naik jika: segel pintu/jendela menua, AC tidak efisien ↓ turun jika: insulasi baik, ruang interior ───────────────────────────────────────────────────── Convergence expectation (λ = 0.98, update per 5 mnt): ~50 observasi → ~4 jam → konvergensi awal ~250 observasi → ~21 jam → stabilisasi Asumsi kritis yang perlu divalidasi: Linieritas model dalam θᵢ — valid jika interaksi antar komponen beban kecil. Persistency of excitation: variasi harian φᵢ(t) harus cukup untuk mengeksitasi semua parameter.

"After sufficient observation, each room carries a parameter vector that encodes its actual thermal behavior — not its designed behavior."

05 · MCDM Formulation
Structuring the Trade-off: MCDM as the Decision Language

The energy-comfort trade-off has a well-defined mathematical structure: a bi-objective optimization problem over a discrete set of operational alternatives, evaluated against two criteria that are both dynamic and conflictual. MCDM provides the appropriate framework — not because it resolves the conflict, but because it makes the trade-off explicit, quantifiable, and context-dependent rather than implicit and static.

The weighting scheme is not elicited from the operator at runtime. It is structured into the system through temporal weighting of IHC — reflecting the operational judgment that peak hours (11:00–15:00) are disproportionately consequential for occupant experience, representing only ~30% of the operational day but carrying the highest thermal stress. The specific values are design choices that require sensitivity analysis: a change in weighting changes the ranking of alternatives and therefore the recommendations produced.

IHC berbobot temporal
f(h) = |{i : |PMV_i(h)| ≤ 0.5}| / N (fraksi ruang nyaman jam h) IHC = w_p · f̄_peak + w_n · f̄_non-peak f̄_peak = (1/|H_peak|) · Σ_{h ∈ H_peak} f(h) f̄_non-peak = (1/|H_non|) · Σ_{h ∈ H_non} f(h) w_p = 0.30, H_peak = {11, 12, 13, 14, 15} w_n = 0.70, H_non = {7, 8, 9, 10, 16, 17} Output per ruang: rec_i = argmax_{a ∈ A} [ ΔPMV_i(a), ΔAC_load_i(a) ] → keduanya dilaporkan ke operator sebelum komitmen

06 · Digital Twin & Computational Cost
The Medium and Its Cost: Spatialization Is Not Free

The digital twin in this system serves a specific function: it is the evaluation engine through which the MCDM framework assesses consequences before a decision is committed. The 3D spatial visualization is secondary — a medium for making room-level decisions spatially intelligible to the operator, not a contribution in itself.

The digital twin literature has largely focused on demonstrating feasibility — building systems that work — rather than analyzing the computational cost of their operation at scale. Most demonstrator systems operate at coarse granularity (building or zone level) where computational load is manageable. The situation changes fundamentally when the unit of analysis shifts to the room level, update frequency increases to sub-hourly, and the model must adapt continuously from streaming sensor data.

Three dimensions of computational complexity interact multiplicatively — not additively. Increasing spatial resolution multiplies the cost of every temporal update. Adaptive learning adds a background burden that competes with foreground recommendation serving. Characterizing this efficiency-accuracy frontier empirically, for a 110-room educational building, is a contribution the literature has not yet made.
